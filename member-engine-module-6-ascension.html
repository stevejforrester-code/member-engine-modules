MODULE 6

Ascension

Give Your Best Members Somewhere to Go

The Retention Problem

"Here is the retention paradox nobody talks about: your most successful members are most likely to cancel."

A member joins to lose 20 pounds. They lose 20 pounds. Mission accomplished. They cancel. A member joins to hit $10K MRR. They hit it. Cancel. A member joins to master a skill. They master it. Cancel.

This is the Graduation Problem. When members achieve their initial goal and leave because they have outgrown you. And it is costing you your best, most engaged, highest-value members.

No Ascension Path

$1,200
$50/month for 2 years, then graduation churn

With Ascension Path

$12,000+
Upgrades to $200/month, stays 5 years

The Misconception

Most membership owners think ascension is about making more money. It is not. It is about churn prevention. Members who have nowhere to go... leave. Ascension creates a growth path within your ecosystem so members never outgrow you. They just grow deeper with you.

The Core Principle

The Big Idea

Retention through expansion. Keeping members engaged long-term requires giving them somewhere to go without leaving. Ascension creates a growth path that turns 12-month relationships into decade-long partnerships. Stop thinking "How do I keep someone for 12 months?" Start thinking "How do I serve this person for 5 to 10 years?"

When Members Achieve Their Initial Goal, They Face Three Options

Option 1: Cancel

Mission accomplished, no reason to stay. The most common outcome without an ascension path.

Option 2: Coast

Stay subscribed but disengage. Eventually cancel anyway. Worse than Option 1 because you don't see it coming.

Option 3: Ascend

Redefine the mission. Deepen the relationship. This is the outcome a well-designed ascension path makes irresistible.

Why Ascenders Stay

Members who ascend are more invested (sunk cost plus increasing value), more engaged (they are getting more), more committed (they chose to deepen the relationship), and far less likely to leave due to higher relational switching costs. Ascension increases the cost of leaving through genuine value expansion, not manipulation.

The Science: Two Key Concepts

The Graduation Problem (and How to Solve It)

When members achieve their stated goal, they mentally "graduate" and cancel, even if you have more to offer. Goal completion triggers a sense of closure. "Mission accomplished" feels like permission to leave.

The solution: Redefine the mission before they achieve the initial goal.

Example: Fitness Membership

Member goal: "Lose 20 pounds." At 15 pounds lost: "Amazing progress! You are on track to hit 20. Let's talk about what's next: maintaining this forever, or pushing to your ideal body composition?" New goal: "Maintain 160 lbs and build lean muscle." Result: They don't cancel. They ascend.

The reframe: The initial goal is not the destination. It is Stage 1. Stage 2 was always part of the journey.

Net Negative Churn: The Holy Grail Metric

When expansion revenue (upgrades, upsells, add-ons) exceeds churn revenue, your MRR grows even without a single new member.

Example: 100 members at $50/month
Month 1: 100 x $50 = $5,000 MRR
Month 2: 5 cancel (-$250)
Month 2: 10 upgrade to $200 (+$1,500 net expansion)
Net result: +$1,250 MRR despite 5% member churn

Most memberships fight churn by adding new members to replace lost ones. That is a treadmill. With negative churn, you are growing revenue from your existing base. That is compounding.

The Goal

Make expansion revenue exceed churn revenue every single month. This is what Robert Skrob means when he says correctly handled ascension can increase LTV by 3 to 10x.

Four Types of Ascension

Not all ascension is the same. Choose the types that fit your membership model and your members' natural next steps.

Vertical

More of the Same

Same offering, higher intensity or frequency.

Basic (1 call/month) → Pro (4 calls/month) → VIP (unlimited)

Works when: Members want more of what you already provide.

Horizontal

Adjacent Specialisations

Master one area, expand into related ones at the same level.

Weight loss program → Strength program → Athletic performance

Works when: Members master one area and want to expand.

Depth

Add-On Services

Same tier with optional extras layered on top.

Course + implementation calls ($100 add-on) / Platform + setup service ($500 one-time)

Works when: Members want help implementing, not just learning.

Adjacent

Experiences and Credentials

One-time experiential or credential products.

Monthly membership → Annual conference ($1,500) / Learning platform → Certification to teach it

Works when: Members want identity or experiential next steps.

Combine Types for Maximum LTV

MasterClass uses horizontal ascension only (one price, all topics). It works but caps per-member revenue. Peloton combines vertical (bike upgrade), horizontal (run + cycle), and adjacent (apparel, events). The multi-type approach compounds LTV far beyond what any single type can achieve alone.

Ascension Timing

The right offer at the wrong time is the wrong offer. Timing is everything.

The Ascension Window

Month 1-2: Too Early
They haven't achieved enough to want more. Feels pushy. Damages the trust you just started building.
Month 3-6: Sweet Spot
First major win achieved. Habit formed. Results visible. They believe in the process and are excited about what is next. This is your window.
Month 7-9: Getting Late
Still viable if they are progressing. Beginning to coast if not. Act on the sweet spot signals while you can.
Month 10-12+: Mature or Gone
Either they ascended and are committed, or they are mentally checked out and considering leaving. Very hard to shift at this stage.

Three Tactical Trigger Moments

Early Momentum (Month 1-2)

Use quick wins to introduce annual plans. "You hit your first milestone in 2 weeks! Imagine what you'll achieve in a full year. Lock in the annual plan now and save $200."

Retention Buffer (Month 3 or 6)

Offer upgrades right before known drop-off points. "Month 3 is when most members plateau. Want to make sure you don't? Upgrade to Pro for personalised support."

Achievement Moments (Variable)

When they hit a major milestone. "Congrats on hitting $10K MRR! Ready for the next level? Here's what scaling to $50K looks like."

The Success Path Reframe

Introduce Stage 2 Before They Finish Stage 1

What it is: Showing the full multi-stage journey from Day 1, so there is never a natural graduation point.

Why it works: If the journey is always multi-stage, there is no natural end point where cancellation makes sense. Members cannot graduate from a path that keeps extending.

The Three-Stage Architecture

Fitness Membership

Stage 1 (Months 1-6): Lose 20 lbs

Stage 2 (Months 7-12): Build lean muscle while maintaining weight

Stage 3 (Year 2+): Athletic performance or body composition goals

Business Coaching

Stage 1 (Months 1-6): Hit $10K MRR

Stage 2 (Months 7-18): Scale to $50K MRR

Stage 3 (Year 2+): Build team and systems for $100K+

When to Introduce Each Stage

In Activation (Day 1)

Show the full path from the beginning. "Here's your journey: Stage 1 (Months 1-6) = Foundation. Stage 2 (Months 7-12) = Optimisation. Stage 3 (Year 2+) = Mastery."

At Month 3-4 (Before Stage 1 completion)

"You are crushing Stage 1. Let's start thinking about Stage 2. Here is what that looks like for someone at your level..."

At Stage 1 completion

"Incredible. You hit your goal. Now here is the exciting part: Stage 2 is where you make this permanent and build on everything you've achieved."

Tiered Membership Structure

Good, Better, Best

What it is: Two to four tiers with clear value differentiation, allowing members to self-select based on needs and budget.

Why it works: Members choose their own pace (autonomy), there is a natural upgrade path, and you capture different willingness to pay without excluding anyone.

Foundation

$50-100/month

Core content library and self-paced learning

Group community access

Monthly Q&A calls

Accelerator

$150-300/month

Everything in Foundation, plus:

Monthly 1:1 coaching call

Exclusive workshops and priority support

Advanced content and tools

VIP / Done-For-You

$500-2,000/month

Everything in Accelerator, plus:

Weekly 1:1 coaching and quarterly strategy sessions

Done-for-you implementation

Direct access (Slack / phone)

Naming Matters

Avoid "Basic / Premium / Elite" (hierarchical, shame-inducing). Use mission-aligned names: "Foundation / Accelerator / Mastery" or "Self-Guided / Coached / Done-For-You." The name signals the transformation, not a social ranking.

Pitch Timing

Do not pitch upgrades in the first 30 days. At Month 3: "You are ready for more. Here is what Accelerator unlocks." At first major win: "Imagine what you'd achieve with 1:1 support. Want to explore VIP?"

Add-On Services and Annual Plans

Add-On Services (Depth Ascension)

What it is: A la carte services that enhance the core membership without requiring a full tier upgrade. Lower commitment, immediate revenue.

One-Time Add-Ons

Setup or onboarding call ($200-500)

Strategy session ($300-1,000)

Done-for-you implementation ($1,000-5,000)

Conference or retreat ticket ($500-2,000)

Recurring Add-Ons

Monthly 1:1 coaching calls (+$100-300/month)

Accountability partner matching (+$50/month)

Advanced tool access (+$25-50/month)

Private mastermind group (+$200-500/month)

The Psychology

Add-ons feel like help, not upsells. Members request them when they need them. The framing is "I can help you with that specific problem" rather than "please upgrade your account."

4

Annual Plan Upgrades (Lock-In Ascension)

What it is: Converting monthly members to annual plans. Reduces monthly churn to zero for 12 months. Increases LTV immediately. Creates commitment through sunk cost.

The Pitch at Month 3-6

"You've achieved [result] in 3 months. Imagine what you'll achieve in a full year. Lock in your annual plan now: save $200 and get exclusive bonuses: [bonus 1], [bonus 2], [bonus 3]."

Annual-only bonuses that create genuine urgency: annual planning session (1:1 call), exclusive annual member community, early access to new content, physical welcome kit.

Best timing: Month 3-6 (first major win achieved), right before monthly renewal, or during promotions (Black Friday, New Year).

Why it works: Humans crave status and recognition. Being elevated within the tribe is a powerful reward that operates independently of price, creating a second reason to stay.

Why This Compounds Retention

The Retention Driver

Status ascension requires continued engagement, milestone achievement, and community contribution. Each of those is also a retention behaviour. The status ladder and the retention engine are the same thing.

Timing for Adjacent Offers

Experiences work best at the 6 to 12 month mark (established members with momentum). Certifications work best at 12 to 24 months (mastery achieved). The pitch: "You've mastered the fundamentals. Ready to take it to the next level? Join us for the annual [retreat / certification / intensive]."

The LTV Multiplier Effect

Members stay in the core membership AND buy adjacent products. A member at $100/month who attends a $2,000 retreat and earns a $3,000 certification has a total LTV of over $6,000 in year one alone, without ever upgrading their subscription tier.

The Ethics Check

Ascension Should Feel Like Service, Not Extraction

Ethical ascension is leadership: recognising where a member is in their journey and offering the next step that genuinely serves them. It is not about maximising revenue from every interaction.

Ethical Ascension

Offer ascension when members are ready for more

Each tier delivers genuine incremental value

Members can fully succeed at any tier

Upgrades solve real problems members are experiencing

Pricing and tier differences are transparent from day one

Value Traps to Avoid

Pushing upgrades before members are ready

Artificially crippling the base tier to force upgrades

Making members feel inadequate for staying at lower tiers

Fake scarcity: "Only 3 VIP spots left!" with no real limit

Features that appear available but are paywalled unexpectedly

The Bait-and-Switch Test

  • Sell: "Join for $50/month and achieve [Stage 1 outcome]. When you're ready for Stage 2, here are your options."
  • Sell: "Join for $50/month and achieve [outcome]." Then immediately: "Actually, to really achieve it, you need the $500/month tier."

Ethical ascension offers more. Bait-and-switch implies the initial tier was insufficient. The difference is respect for the original promise.

"Would I feel good about my best friend buying the Foundation tier and staying there forever? If the honest answer is no, I have built a value trap."

Real-World Teardown

Three Ascension Models

Peloton: Vertical + Adjacent

The base tier (Bike + Membership) is fully functional with no value trap. Upgrades offer genuine additional value (larger screen, auto-resistance). Adjacent products (apparel, live events) deepen identity and make members Pelotoners before they are cyclists. Status system with milestone rides and leaderboard ranks creates social ascension alongside financial. Average LTV grows from $1,495 to $3,000-5,000+ through this combination.

MasterClass: Horizontal Only

Single price tier, all topics, no vertical tiers. Ascension is purely horizontal: cooking leads to photography leads to writing. It works and simplifies the decision. The limitation is that everyone pays the same regardless of usage or value received, which leaves revenue uncaptured from power users. Lesson: horizontal ascension works, but combining it with vertical creates significantly more LTV potential per member.

The Full Ecosystem Model

The most durable ascension architectures follow a "What, How, With, For You" structure:

Stage 1 (Months 1-3): Course

Teaches the framework. "Here is what to do." Self-paced, affordable entry point.

Stage 2 (Months 3-6): Membership

Shows how with ongoing support. "Here is how to do it, repeatedly." Monthly community and advanced tactics.

Stage 3 (Months 6-12): Consultancy

Does it with them. "We implement together." High-touch, high-value, done-for-you.

Stage 4 (Year 2+): Tool or Platform

Does it for them automatically. "Set it and it runs." The logical top of the ladder once they have the foundation.

At every stage, there is a next step. Members never graduate because the ladder keeps extending.
