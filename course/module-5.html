DETECTION

Members don't cancel. They ghost. The decision is made weeks before the click. This module is about reading the signals while you can still do something about them.

Module Introduction
VIDEO COMING SOON

THE PROBLEM

"Members don't cancel impulsively. They ghost you first. They mentally check out weeks or months before they ever click cancel."

By the time you see the cancellation, the decision was already made. The credit card has been refunded, the account is closed, and you're left wondering what happened. Spoiler: it happened six weeks ago and you missed all the signs.

The problem is not that you lack data. It is that you are not detecting the signals until it is too late. You're a historian when you need to be a forecaster.

5-7%
Average monthly member loss across most memberships. Every month. Quietly.
40%
Of that churn is involuntary. Failed payments you could have recovered with a single email.
60%
Is voluntary churn from disengaged members you could have saved with early intervention.
THE SHIFT

Detection transforms you from a historian (analysing what already happened) to a forecaster (predicting and preventing what is about to happen). The difference between lagging indicators and leading indicators. One tells you the final score. The other tells you the score at half-time, when you can still do something about it.

THE CORE PRINCIPLE

THE BIG IDEA

Detection is a behaviour-triggered, reactive discipline that monitors member signals to intervene before cancellation occurs. It is the counterpart to Communication Architecture, which is calendar-based and proactive. You need both. One is planned. The other watches for what you didn't plan for.

THE CHURN DECISION PROCESS

Members rarely wake up and impulsively cancel. Churn is the final action in a long disengagement sequence. Here is the sequence. Your job is to interrupt it before it reaches Step 6.

Step 1: Stop logging in as frequently
Step 2: Stop consuming content
Step 3: Stop engaging with peers
Step 4: Mental decision: "I am not getting value"
Step 5: Trigger event: Credit card charge hits
Step 6: Final action: Cancel
YOUR INTERVENTION WINDOW IS BETWEEN STEPS 3 AND 4. THAT IS WHERE DETECTION LIVES.
THE MINDSET SHIFT

Stop asking "Why did they cancel?" Start asking "What signals did I miss three weeks before they cancelled?" The first question is useful for a post-mortem. The second question is useful for running a business.

LEADING VS LAGGING INDICATORS

Most membership owners only track lagging metrics. These tell you the score after the game is already over. The team that wins is the one watching the leading metrics while they still have time to adjust.

THE SIX STRATEGIES

01
CHURN PREDICTION: BUILD YOUR EARLY WARNING SYSTEM

What it is: Identifying at-risk members before they ever think about cancelling, using behavioural signals to score engagement.

Why it works: Intervening when someone is thinking about cancelling is 10x easier than winning them back after they have already left. You are catching it at Week -6, not trying to reverse it at Week 0.

02
INTERVENTION PLAYBOOKS

What it is: Pre-built responses triggered by specific behavioural signals. Automated or manual, depending on your scale. The key word is "pre-built." You decide what to do before the crisis, not during it.

Why it works: Proactive outreach before they decide to cancel shows you noticed them. Most members re-engage simply because someone noticed them. That's it. That's the whole strategy.

PLAYBOOK A: THE "WE MISS YOU" NUDGE
Trigger
No login in 14 days
Action
Personalised email or SMS
Message
"Hey [Name], we noticed you haven't been around lately. Here's what you missed... and here's your next step: [link]"
Bonus
Attach a "catch up in 5 minutes" resource to reduce re-entry friction. The barrier to returning is higher than the barrier to staying. Lower it.
PLAYBOOK B: THE ONBOARDING RESCUE
Trigger
30 days since signup, onboarding still incomplete
Action
Personal video message or a genuine 1:1 call offer
Message
"I see you haven't completed [first action] yet. Can I help you get unstuck? Here's a 5-minute walkthrough."
PLAYBOOK C: THE HAPPINESS BOMB
Trigger
Month 3: the typical drop-off point, not a coincidence
Action
Unexpected surprise: physical postcard, bonus content, or exclusive access
Message
"You've been with us for 3 months! Here's a little thank you..." (no ask, just give)
PLAYBOOK D: THE HIGH-VALUE VIP SAVE
Trigger
High-LTV member showing disengagement signals
Action
Personal phone call or video from you directly
Why
For members worth $500 or more in LTV, 15 minutes of your time can save thousands in revenue. The maths is embarrassingly obvious once you do it.
03
DUNNING SEQUENCES: RECOVER 40% OF YOUR CHURN

What it is: Automated communication to recover failed credit card payments before they result in cancellation.

Why it works: Most failed payments are not intentional cancellations. Expired cards, insufficient funds, bank declines. You can recover 70-80% of these with a well-structured dunning sequence. This is the most straightforward revenue recovery in the whole module.

WRONG FRAMING

"Your payment failed."

Accusatory. Puts the blame on the member. Creates defensiveness at exactly the moment you need openness.

RIGHT FRAMING

"We're missing some info."

Collaborative. Takes ownership. Invites the member to solve it together rather than defend themselves.

THE DUNNING SEQUENCE
DAY 0
Subject: "We're missing some info for your account"
"Hey [Name], looks like we're missing some billing info. Can you update your card here? [Link]. No worries if it's just an expired card. That happens all the time!"
DAY 3
Smart retry + reminder
Auto-retry on likely payday. Email: "Quick reminder, we still need your updated card info. Here's a 1-click link: [Link]"
DAY 7
Final attempt before pause
"Hey [Name], your account will be paused in 48 hours. Update here to keep your access: [Link]. Need help? [Phone / live chat]"
PRE-EMPTIVE
30 days before card expiry
"Your card ending in [1234] expires soon. Update now to avoid any interruption to your access." Most platforms can trigger this automatically. Use it.
TECH STACK NOTE

Most payment processors (Stripe, Paddle) have built-in smart retry logic. Tools like Churn Buster or ProfitWell Retain add advanced dunning on top. Start with what your processor already provides before buying additional tools. (This advice applies more broadly than just dunning.)

04
WIN-BACK CAMPAIGNS

What it is: Targeted sequences to reactivate members who have already cancelled.

Why it works: Churned members require far less education than cold prospects. They already know what you offer. They've already made one decision to buy. You're asking them to make a second one, not a first.

SEGMENT BY EXIT REASON
PRICE

Win-back offer: 30% off for 3 months to return

Message: Acknowledge the constraint, make it affordable

NOT ENOUGH TIME

Win-back offer: "We added flexible catch-up weeks and async options since you left"

Message: Show how the product now fits their actual life

NOT GETTING RESULTS

Win-back offer: Free 1:1 session to build a personal success path

Message: Take responsibility. Offer hands-on help. Don't get defensive.

FOUND A COMPETITOR

Win-back offer: "Here is what we've launched since you left"

Message: Show innovation, create genuine FOMO about what they're missing

WIN-BACK SEQUENCE: 4 EMAILS OVER 3 MONTHS
Week 1: Exit Survey
"We're sorry to see you go. Can you tell us why?" If they didn't complete one during cancellation, this is your last chance to understand what went wrong.
Week 2: Emotional Connection
"We miss you." No hard sell. Just a genuine human touchpoint. This sounds soft. It is actually quite effective because nobody else does it.
Month 1: What's New
"Here's what has changed since you left." Feature updates, member wins, new additions. Shows the product is alive and improving.
Month 3: Comeback Offer
"Special offer: 30% off to rejoin." Limited time. Creates urgency without desperation. This is your last contact. Make it count.
PRIORITISE YOUR WIN-BACK EFFORT

During cancellation, ask: "How likely are you to return? (1-10)." Scores of 8 to 10 are high priority. Scores of 1 to 4 are not worth your energy. Do not spend time convincing people who have genuinely moved on. Spend it on people who might come back.

05
THE PAUSE AND THE STORE CREDIT PIVOT

Two interventions at the exact point of cancellation. Both convert members who were about to leave into members who stay. Both are genuinely easy to implement. Most people don't do either.

THE PAUSE OPTION

Offer a pause for 1 to 3 months before completing cancellation. Frame it as a break, not a breakup. They keep their account, their progress, and their grandfathered pricing. You keep the member long-term.

Step 1: Exit Survey
"Before you go, can you tell us why you're cancelling?" Too expensive / Not enough time / Not getting results / Found a better option / Other. One click each. Takes six seconds. Gives you everything.
Step 2: Contextual Offer
If too expensive: "Would pausing for 1 to 3 months help? You keep your account, progress, and grandfathered pricing." If not enough time: "Life gets busy. Pause for up to 3 months and come back when you're ready." If not getting results: "Would a free 1:1 call help? Or pause while you catch up."
Step 3: If They Still Cancel
Make it easy. FTC compliance requires it. Set an auto-reminder two weeks before their pause ends if they chose to pause. Catch them before they forget they're on pause.
THE STORE CREDIT PIVOT

When a member requests a refund, offer double the value in store credit instead of cash. Keeps them inside your ecosystem. Resolves the financial objection without losing them permanently.

THE SCRIPT

Member: "I'd like a refund."

You: "Absolutely, I can process that. Quick question: would you be open to double the value in store credit instead? That way you can try [other product] or use it when timing is better. But if you need the cash refund, totally understand."

  • They feel heard (you honoured the refund request without refusing)
  • You offered a genuinely better deal (2x value)
  • You retained the customer relationship
  • Reported to reduce refunds by 33% or more when consistently applied

When not to use it: Members citing serious service failures. Just refund those. No pivot. Some situations call for a clean exit, not a retention tactic.

THE TOP 3 CHURN DRIVERS
1. Consistent Low Engagement and Platform Inactivity
The biggest driver by far. Members who do not engage regularly are far more likely to cancel. This is your engagement scoring signal. Not a surprise. What surprises people is how early it starts showing up.
2. Multiple Failed Payments
Involuntary churn that should be near-fully recoverable with a good dunning sequence. If you are losing members this way, it is an operational fix, not a product one. Fix it once and it runs itself.
3. Lack of Email Communication Engagement
Members who stop opening your emails have mentally checked out. Email disengagement predicts cancellation by 4 to 6 weeks. This is the Week -6 signal. If you track nothing else, track this one.

THE ETHICS CHECK

RETENTION VS DETENTION

Detection strategies must remain ethical. You are building a Forever Transaction based on trust, not engineering friction to trap members who want to leave. The pause offer is a helpful option. The guilt-trip survey is a manipulative barrier. The difference matters and members can feel it.

COMPLIANT OFFBOARDING: THE FTC CLICK TO CANCEL RULE
  • Cancel process is no harder than the sign-up process
  • One-click cancel if they signed up with one click
  • Exit survey is optional, not a mandatory gate before cancellation
  • Pause offer is presented as a helpful option, not a manipulative barrier
  • Force phone calls to cancel when they signed up online
  • Endless "Are you sure?" screens stacked to block cancellation
  • Hidden cancel buttons or fake errors during the cancellation flow
TRANSPARENT DUNNING
  • Contact members about failed payments before they contact you
  • Use collaborative, non-accusatory language throughout
  • Offer genuine help (live chat, phone) for technical payment issues
  • Make members feel ashamed about a failed payment
  • Use urgent or threatening language in billing communications
LEGAL REALITY
Obstruction of cancellation is not just unethical. In many jurisdictions it is now illegal. Build the easy exit before regulators force you to under worse circumstances. The reputational damage when dark patterns are exposed publicly is far worse than any members you would have "retained." It is not a close call.
